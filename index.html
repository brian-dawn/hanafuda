How to play Koi-Koi

Object

Match cards by their month (the plant on the card) to capture them into your pile. Certain capture combinations are yaku (scoring patterns). After each yaku you form, decide whether to stop (Agari) and lock in your score, or gamble on more by calling Koi-Koi.

Turn

  1. Play one card from your hand. If it matches a field card's month, you capture both. If 2 field cards match, you pick one.
  2. Flip the top of the deck. Same rule: match one field card and capture; if it doesn't match, it lies on the field.
  3. If you just formed a new yaku, you are prompted: Agari (score now) or Koi-Koi (continue — but if your opponent scores next, their points double).

Card types

Each month has four cards, divided into four categories:

  • Hikari (Brights, 5 in deck) — for Gokō / Shikō / Sankō.
  • Tane (Animals, 9 in deck) — 5+ = 1 pt, +1 each extra.
  • Tan (Ribbons, 10 in deck) — 5+ = 1 pt, +1 each extra.
  • Kasu (Chaff, 24 in deck) — 10+ = 1 pt, +1 each extra.

Any yaku total of 7+ is doubled. If either player called Koi-Koi during the hand, the final score is also doubled (they stack).

Tips

  • Hover a card (or tap-and-hold on mobile) to see its role in yaku.
  • Call Koi-Koi when you're behind or have a clear path to a 7+ yaku.
  • Agari when your opponent is close to scoring or your hand is running out.
